Heidelberg is a popular tourist destinations. At the heart is the old city (Altstadt) where you can find the castle, the old bridge and many other attractions. Most of the area is pedestrain with the Hauptstrasse being one of the longest pedestrian streets in Europe.

The Main Attractions

Credit: Pixabay and Heidelbergerin

The big thing in Heidelberg is the castle. You can either walk up there or get the Bergbahn railway up. The latter leaves from Kornmarkt and you can buy a combi castle + Bergbahn ticket at the station there. The castle has a 219,000 litre wine barrel and a pharmacy museum.

Further Afield

Luisenpark in Mannheim is about 35 mins away on the number 5 tram. Credit: Pixabay

The area around Heidelberg has a lot of great places to visit. For those with families Luisenpark in Mannheim is great. It has loads of different animals and wonderful flora. To the east the Odenwald is a great place for hiking. Schwetzingen is about 20 mins away on the bus and has a castle with a stunning formal garden. To the south Hockenheim has the famous Hockenheimring racetrack. They do daily tours. There's also the engineering museums at Speyer and Sinsheim (the latter has a Concorde and a Concordski).

If you fancy watching some sport Sinsheim is the home of Bundesliga club Hoffenheim (season may not have started on 1st of August) and the small village of Sandhausen to the south has a Bundesliga 2 team (season probably will have started).
