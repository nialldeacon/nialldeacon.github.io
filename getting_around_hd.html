Getting around Heidelberg

By Public Transport

The older part of the Bergbahn that goes up the hill next to Heidelberg. Credit: Pixabay and M W

The nearest large airport to Heidelberg is Frankurt. This is a major hub for Lufthansa and is served by most major intercontinental airlines. From the UK you can fly from Edinburgh, Glasgow, Manchester, Brimingham and Heathrow. The only budget airline that flies there from the UK currently (2019) is Ryanair from Stanstead. From Aberdeen you would need to change at either Schiphol, CDG or Heathrow. It's easy to get from Frankfurt Airport to Heidelberg, there's a high-speed train (ICE) to Mannheim where you change to a 15min local train to Heidelberg

There are regular flights to Stanstead and the last few summers have had a flight from Ediburgh twice a week. You could hire a car at Baden or get the bus to Radsdat and a train from there via Karlsruhe to Heidelberg. Hahn used to have regular flights to Edinburgh and Stanstead but those have stopped. Hahn isn't that close to Frankfurt so don't go to Heidelberg via Frankfurt. Instead rent a car or take a long distance bus.

Stuttgart has flights to Edinburgh and Gatwick with Easyjet as well as Heathrow (BA & Eurowings) and Birmingham (Flybe). The airport is on the Stuttgart local train system so just get a train to Stuttgart main station and then an intercity to Heidelberg.

By car

Traffic in Bismarkplatz, the busiest part of the city. Credit: Pixabay and zoe sartain

Getting round Heidelberg by car isn't great. There are a couple of big parking garages. One under Darmstadterhof near Bismarkplatz, one below Kornmarkt below the castle and a few along Freidrich Ebert Anlage (one under Freidrich Ebert Platz). However on a Saturday these are often full.

To get to the venue from Heidelberg you'll need to head along the southern side of the River Neckar then go up the hill once you get to Schlierbach. From the west side of town you can take the tunnels from Adernauerplatz that byepass the old city for a quicker journey
